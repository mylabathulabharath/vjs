PENUGONDA KAJJIKAYALU

A traditional Andhra sweet prepared by making thin puris with balls of maida dough and filling them with a mixture of coconut, sugar and cardamom powder before frying. This speciality is quite similar to a north Indian gujiya, which is prepared on the festivals.

RECIPE:

  • The pastry dough – incorporate hot oil into the flour before kneading it with water. This ensures that the pastry is crisp when fried.
  • Add semolina to the pastry dough also ensures that it stays crisp and flaky with longer shelf life
  • As the pastry is made with maida, it should rest for a good 15-30 mins to get it softer.
  • The pastry should be flaky, crispy and golden - it is should be fried in low to medium flame. Keep the oil on lowest heat when dropping the pastry into hot oil. Fry on low heat until they are golden brown.
  • Fill the pastry with as much stuffing as possible (about 2 tbsp) and seal it with water tightly before forming the shape. Without proper sealing, the stuffing can spill out when deep-fried.
  • Oil temperature should be maintained at medium to low at all times. At high heat, the sweet changes color quickly but does not retain the crispness for long.
  • Always wait until the fried goodies are at room temperature before stacking them in airtight containers.

NATTU KODI PULUSU

country chicken is considered to be healthy in comparison to broiler chicken. This curry tastes good with Ragi Sankati, a foxtail millet preparation and is very famous in Rayalseema region.

RECIPE:

  • Clean and wash chicken. Add turmeric, ginger garlic paste and juice of half lemon. Mix well
  • Put the marinated chicken in cooker and add one cup of water. Cook it till 8 whistles Fry the dry masala.
  • Grind it along with a tomato Grind it to a smooth paste
  • Heat oil in a pan. Add turmeric and curry leaves. Add chillies, let them fry
  • Add sliced onions once chillies are fried Add salt once onion changes colour. Mix well.
  • Add ginger garlic paste. Mix well. Let it cook for 3-4mins till the raw smell goes Add the grinded paste and mix well
  • Open the cooker and keep the chicken ready Add the chicken along with the stock Add red chilli powder and water if required, mix well.
  • Cook for 15mins with a lid on Garnish with fresh coriander and serve it with Ragi Sangati or white rice or jowar roti or chapathi.
